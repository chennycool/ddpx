Introduction

The body mass index (BMI) or Quetelet index is a value derived from the mass (weight) and height of an individual. The BMI is an attempt to quantify the amount of tissue mass (muscle, fat, and bone) in an individual, and then categorize that person as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese based on that value.

The Algorithm

Weight<- 65
Height<- 1.70
BMI<- (Weight) / (Height^2)
BMI

## [1] 22.49135

The BMI is defined as the body mass divided by the square of the body height, resulting from mass in kilograms and height in metres.
